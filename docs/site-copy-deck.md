# Soni Transfer Website Copy Deck

Status: English draft for review

## Copy Strategy

Each page has one job. Supporting claims should appear only where they help that job.

| Page | Primary job | Main idea reserved for this page |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Home | Orient and start | See what your recipient gets |
| How it works | Explain the journey | What happens before, during, and after payment |
| Rates & fees | Explain price | Rate, fee, receive amount, and possible third-party costs |
| Cash pickup | Prepare for collection | What the recipient needs at the payout location |
| Bank deposit | Prevent bank-detail errors | Supported account details and deposit expectations |
| Mobile wallet | Prepare a wallet transfer | Eligible number, provider, and wallet status |
| Airtime & Cash Power | Explain non-cash support | Phone and electricity top-ups |
| About | Explain the company | Who Soni serves and why the service exists |
| Compliance & security | Build informed trust | Verification, monitoring, regulation, and customer responsibility |
| Help & FAQs | Resolve common questions | Self-service answers and next steps |
| Contact | Route a specific issue | What to send and where to escalate |
| Blog | Show community involvement | Events, partnerships, and community stories |

## Repetition Removed

- Use "simple", "easy", "reliable", and "accessible" only when the page proves the claim. Do not stack them as generic adjectives.
- Keep the full payout-method list on the homepage and Help page only. Individual service pages discuss their own method.
- Keep the three-step overview on the homepage. The How It Works page uses a detailed journey rather than repeating the same three cards.
- Keep regulation details on Compliance & Security and in the footer/legal pages. Other pages may link there without repeating the FCA paragraph.
- Keep rate and fee explanations on Rates & Fees. Service pages say that the total is shown before payment without repeating the pricing section.
- Use "support loved ones" sparingly. Prefer the specific customer task on service and help pages.
- Do not place the same download banner at the bottom of every page. Use a page-specific next action.

## How It Works

Eyebrow: The transfer journey

H1: What happens when you send with Soni

Intro: From account checks to payout confirmation, here is what to expect at each stage of a transfer.

Key points:

- Verify your identity before sending
- Add accurate recipient information
- Review the final price before payment

Journey sections:

1. **Set up your account** — Create an account and provide the information needed to verify your identity. We may request additional documents where required.
2. **Add your recipient** — Enter the recipient's details exactly as they appear on their identification, bank account, or wallet account.
3. **Choose how they receive it** — Available receiving methods are shown for the selected destination and recipient.
4. **Review before paying** — Check the amount sent, fee, exchange rate, and expected receive amount. Nothing is submitted until you confirm.
5. **Track the transfer** — View status changes in the app. We will let you know if more information is required.

## Blog Article Editing Notes

The three existing posts cover different events and should keep their event-specific facts. During editorial revision:

- Remove generic claims about seamless transfers, strong rates, speed, and trust unless the article directly proves them.
- Open with the event, date, location, and Soni Transfer's role.
- Use one paragraph for what happened, one for why Soni participated, and one for acknowledgement or next steps.
- Avoid ending every article with a statement that Soni is proud, committed, or more than a money-transfer company.
